What is architecture?

Architecture is a multifaceted discipline that goes beyond the construction of structures; it is a form of art that blends creativity and utility. Its basic principles include considering the function and utility of a building, paying attention to aesthetics and form to create visually appealing designs, ensuring the structure and stability for safety, and integrating the building carefully with its surrounding environment, considering geographical and cultural factors.

Why study here?

Studying architecture at Universidad Rey Juan Carlos (URJC) provides a comprehensive education that includes theoretical and practical aspects of architectural design, as well as skills in urban planning and environmental sustainability. Students learn to create environments that are aesthetically pleasing, energy-efficient, and socially relevant.

This is an online platform designed exclusively for students and teachers passionate about the world of architecture. In this space, you can immerse yourself in a collaborative environment that fosters creativity, learning, and innovation.

Basic Information

Building Project and Construction Management:

Knowledge for the conception, planning, and execution of new construction projects and the intervention in existing buildings, considering technical, artistic, human, and economic factors. Familiarity with the methodology of building project development, as well as construction management and supervision.

Urban Planning, Sociology, and the City:

Understanding the various forms of urban planning and their technical, artistic, social, and managerial development. Design, planning, and execution of urban spaces, gardens, industrial estates, and, in general, territorial organization at different scales, considering technical, human, artistic, and economic factors.

Architecture, Business, and Management:

Understanding both the regulations applicable to the practice of the architect profession and the organization and management of professional activities. Knowledge of management systems, real estate appraisal, and valuation. Understanding of economics and business management essential for the development of professional and entrepreneurial activities. Awareness of the architect's capabilities to broaden their range of employment opportunities. Knowledge of current legislation and the functioning of professional associations.

Construction, Structural Calculation, and Materials:

Understanding the factors that affect construction, urban planning, and, in general, the planning and execution of spaces and their technical solutions. Knowledge of materials, geotechnics, conception, design, and calculation of structures, conception, design, and calculation of installations, pathology in construction, and rehabilitation and heritage management.

Design, Art, and Space:

Understanding the history of art and architecture. Knowledge of techniques and graphic resources for the conception and spatial representation, both real and virtual: from analytical and conceptual drawing to the final technical representation for execution or communicative purposes, such as competitions or client presentations. Familiarity with design principles in general, including graphic design, and its application to architecture. Conceptualization of exhibition spaces, scenography, and ephemeral architecture.

Architecture brings together knowledge from the arts, science, technology, and social sciences. The thematic areas addressed in this degree are:

  • Building Project and Construction Management.
  • Urban Planning, Sociology, and the City.
  • Architecture, Business, and Management.
  • Construction, Structural Calculation, and Materials.
  • Design, Art, and Space.

  • The maximum duration for students in undergraduate studies is eight years for full-time students. Part-time students may request an extension of up to two additional years from the Rector.
  • For degree programs exceeding 240 credits (4 years), the maximum mentioned above will be extended by one more year for every 60 ECTS credits added to the initial 240 ECTS.
  • Students must pass a minimum of two courses in the first academic year. Part-time students are required to pass at least one course in their first academic year.
  • Students pursuing any official undergraduate degree at Universidad Rey Juan Carlos may enroll a maximum of four times for the completion of each course in the curriculum, excluding any previous cancellations of the same course.

GENERAL COMPETENCIES

  • CG1  That students have demonstrated possession and understanding of knowledge in an area of study that builds on the foundation of general secondary education, typically at a level that, while supported by advanced textbooks, also includes some aspects that involve knowledge from the forefront of their field of study.
  • CG2  That students can apply their knowledge to their work or vocation in a professional manner and possess the competencies usually demonstrated through the development and defense of arguments and the resolution of problems within their area of study.
  • CG3  That students have the ability to gather and interpret relevant data (usually within their field of study) to make judgments that include reflection on significant social, scientific, or ethical issues.
  • CG4  That students can communicate information, ideas, problems, and solutions to both specialized and non-specialized audiences.
  • CG5  That students have developed the learning skills necessary to undertake further studies with a high degree of autonomy.
  • CG6  Understanding the history and theories of architecture, as well as the arts, technologies, and human sciences related to it.
  • CG7  Understanding the role of fine arts as a factor that can influence the quality of architectural conception.
  • CG8  Understanding urban planning and the techniques applied in the planning process.
  • CG9  Understanding the issues of structural design, construction, and engineering associated with building projects, as well as the techniques for resolving them.
  • CG10  Understanding the physical issues, various technologies, and the function of buildings in order to provide them with internal conditions of comfort and protection against climatic factors.
  • CG11  Understanding the industries, organizations, regulations, and procedures to materialize projects into buildings and integrate plans into the overall planning.
  • CG12  Understanding the relationships between people and buildings, as well as between buildings and their surroundings. Recognizing the need to relate buildings and the spaces between them based on human needs and scale.
